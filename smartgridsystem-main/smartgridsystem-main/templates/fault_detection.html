Fault Detection Help
About Fault Detection

The fault detection system monitors the electrical grid for abnormal conditions and potential issues. It helps grid operators identify and address problems before they cause widespread outages.

Types of Detected Faults
  • Frequency Deviation: Grid frequency outside normal operating range (49.7-50.3 Hz).
  • High Load: Regions or substations experiencing load levels approaching capacity limits.
  • Voltage Fluctuation: Unstable voltage levels that could damage equipment or cause instability.
  • Line Loading: Transmission lines carrying loads near their rated capacity.
  • Substation Issues: Problems detected at specific substations.
Severity Levels
  • Critical: Requires immediate attention. High risk of cascading failures or outages.
  • High: Significant issue that should be addressed promptly.
  • Medium: Moderate issue that should be monitored closely.
  • Low: Minor issue that should be logged but may not require immediate action.
Interpreting the Map

The grid fault map shows the geographical distribution of detected issues:

  • Red markers indicate critical faults
  • Orange markers indicate high severity issues
  • Blue markers indicate medium severity issues
  • Gray markers indicate low severity issues

Click on any marker to see detailed information about the fault.
