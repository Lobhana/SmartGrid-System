How Load Balancing Works
About Load Balancing

Load balancing is the process of distributing power between different regions of the electrical grid to maintain stability and efficiency. It helps to:

  • Prevent overloading of transmission lines
  • Maintain frequency stability across the grid
  • Efficiently utilize power generation resources
  • Respond to unexpected demand changes or generation outages
Simulation Scenarios
  • Normal: Standard operating conditions with typical generation and consumption patterns.
  • Peak Demand: Increased consumption across regions, simulating peak hours or extreme weather conditions.
  • Renewable Surplus: Excess generation in some regions due to favorable conditions for renewable energy.
  • Generation Outage: Sudden loss of generation capacity in one region, requiring emergency balancing.
How the Simulation Works
  1. The system analyzes the initial balance in each region (generation minus consumption).
  2. It identifies surplus regions (positive balance) and deficit regions (negative balance).
  3. The algorithm optimally transfers power from surplus to deficit regions.
  4. Transfers are limited by inter-regional transmission capacity.
  5. The system recalculates regional balances and estimates frequency changes.

In a real power grid, this process happens continuously through automatic generation control systems and is coordinated by grid operators.
